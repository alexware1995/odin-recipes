Filet Mignon Steak

The Perfect Filet Mignon

Filet mignon, a slice of beef cut from the small, narrow end of the tenderloin, generally the most expensive steak on the market. Located at the top of a cow or steer just ahead of the hind legs, the tenderloin has few connective tissues and carries no weight, earning it the tender portion of its name. The filet mignon, whose name derives from the French phrase meaning “delicate” or “dainty” fillet, is the most tender part of the tenderloin. Other valued cuts of meat from the tenderloin include chateaubriand and part of a porterhouse or T-bone steak.

Ingrediants

  • Cut of filet mignon
  • Olive oil
  • Garlic
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Butter

Step by Step Instructions

  1. Heat frying pan with olive oil until oil begins to smoke
  2. Season your filet mignon with salt and pepper
  3. When olive oil begins to smoke add butter and garlic to pan
  4. Place steak in frying pan for 5 minutes
  5. After 5 minutes, flip your steak over and let cook for another 5 minutes
  6. Remove steak from pan
